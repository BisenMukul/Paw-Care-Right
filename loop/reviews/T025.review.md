## AC2 — CTA above the fold (vacuity analysis)

Two complementary assertions, both in the online loaded state:

**(a) Structural** (`:192`): gets `pet-home-scroll`, asserts `within(scroll).queryByTestId("pet-home-cta")` is `null` AND root CTA resolves AND `headerRegionIndex < scrollIndex` in DOM order. In `app/pets/[id].tsx` the CTA lives inside `pet-home-header-region` (`:77-93`) rendered OUTSIDE and BEFORE `<ScrollView testID="pet-home-scroll">` (`:94`). The CTA is structurally un-scrollable.

**(b) Budget, non-vacuous** (`:212`): imports the constants module and asserts `ABOVE_FOLD_BUDGET <= Dimensions.get("window").height` (256 ≤ 568), THEN renders and binds the arithmetic to actually-applied styles:
- `StyleSheet.flatten(headerCard.props.style).height === HEADER_CARD_HEIGHT` — component applies `style={{ height: HEADER_CARD_HEIGHT }}` (`pet-header-card.tsx:23`).
- `StyleSheet.flatten(cta.props.style).minHeight === CTA_HEIGHT` — component applies `style={{ minHeight: CTA_HEIGHT }}` (`[id].tsx:88`).

**Verdict on vacuity: NON-VACUOUS.** The two dominant leaf constants (`HEADER_CARD_HEIGHT` 140 + `CTA_HEIGHT` 56 = 196 of 256) are the SAME values asserted as the header-card's and CTA's applied styles — the test cannot pass on invented literals decoupled from the UI. The remaining reserves (`SAFE_AREA_TOP_RESERVE` 44, `SECTION_GAP` 16) only inflate the budget (make the ≤568 test stricter), so they cannot weaken the assertion. Any executor attempt to shrink an applied style away from the constant breaks the style-equality checks; any attempt to inflate a constant to force the fold still yields a genuinely-fitting on-device height. This clears the "constants I made up sum below a number I made up" bar. Limitation R3 (no real Yoga pass) is documented and deferred to the M2 manual checklist — acceptable for a headless assertion.
